01 — Overview

Meaning & Definition

"

A Company Secretary is a senior officer responsible for the efficient administration of a company, ensuring compliance with statutory and regulatory requirements, and implementing decisions made by the Board of Directors.

— Companies Act, 2013 | Section 2(24)
⚖️

Legal & Compliance Officer

Ensures all statutory obligations under the Companies Act are fulfilled and the company operates within the law.

🏛️

Corporate Governance Pillar

Advises the Board of Directors on governance best practices and ensures transparent operations for all stakeholders.

📋

Administrative Head

Manages statutory registers, board meeting minutes, filings with ROC, and internal company records.

🔑

Key Managerial Personnel (KMP)

Designated as KMP under Companies Act 2013 for companies with paid-up capital of ₹5 crore or more.

02 — Classification

Types of Company Secretary

Classified based on nature of engagement, company size, and scope of work.

01
Full-Time

Whole-Time CS

Employed full-time by a single company. Mandatory for companies with paid-up share capital of ₹5 crore or more.

  • Classified as Key Managerial Personnel
  • Fixed salary & employment benefits
  • Reports directly to the Board
02
Retained

Part-Time CS

Provides services on a contractual/retainer basis. Suitable for smaller companies with capital below ₹5 crore.

  • May serve multiple companies
  • Cost-effective for small businesses
  • Not classified as KMP
03
Independent

Practicing CS (PCS)

Holds Certificate of Practice from ICSI. Works independently, provides secretarial and legal advisory to multiple clients.

  • Issues Secretarial Audit Reports
  • Represents companies at NCLT
  • Due diligence & M&A support
04
Corporate

Corporate / Institutional CS

Heads an entire secretarial department in large corporations and listed companies, often part of senior management.

  • Manages a team of CS officers
  • Liaises with SEBI, MCA, stock exchanges
  • Involved in high-level strategy
03 — Academic Path
Qualifications & Requirements

Becoming a Company Secretary requires completing a structured professional programme regulated by the Institute of Company Secretaries of India.

Governing Body

ICSI — Institute of Company Secretaries of India

Statutory professional body established under the Company Secretaries Act, 1980. The only body authorised to regulate CS professionals in India.

F
Foundation
CS Foundation Programme
Entry level after 10+2. Covers Business Environment, Legal Aptitude, Business Management & Economics. Duration: 8 months.
E
Executive
CS Executive Programme
After Foundation or graduation. Covers Company Law, Tax Law, Economic & Commercial Law, Financial Management. Min. 9 months study period.
P
Professional
CS Professional Programme
Final stage. Covers Governance, Secretarial Practice, Corporate Restructuring & Advanced Tax. 21 months after Executive.
T
Training
Practical Training (15 months)
Mandatory training under a Practicing CS or a company. Required before receiving the Associate Member (ACS) designation.
✓
Membership
ACS / FCS Designation
Associate CS (ACS) — upon clearing all exams + training. Fellow CS (FCS) — after 5 years of practice as ACS.
04 — Process

Appointment of CS

Governed by Companies Act 2013, Section 203. Formal board-level process with ROC filings.

1
Board Resolution

Board passes a formal resolution authorizing appointment with name, designation & remuneration terms.

2
Verify Qualifications

Company verifies valid ACS/FCS membership of ICSI and all eligibility criteria under Companies Act.

3
Offer Letter & Consent

Formal appointment letter issued; appointee gives written consent confirming acceptance of role.

4
Filing Form MR-1 with ROC

Within 60 days of appointment, Form MR-1 is filed with the Registrar of Companies notifying the CS as KMP.

5
Annual Report Disclosure

Appointment must be disclosed in Board's Report in the company's Annual Report for that financial year.

05 — Functions

Key Duties

📁
Statutory Registers

Maintains registers of members, directors, charges, contracts as required by law.

📨
ROC Filings

Timely filing of Annual Returns, financial statements and e-forms on the MCA portal.

🗓️
Board & General Meetings

Convenes AGMs, EGMs, Board Meetings — sends notices, prepares agenda, records minutes.

🔏
Secretarial Audit

Conducts or facilitates secretarial audits; issues Secretarial Audit Reports in Form MR-3.

🤝
Advisory to Board

Advises the Board on corporate governance, legal obligations and compliance best practices.

06 — Core Responsibilities

Responsibilities & Thank You

📊

Shareholder Communication

Point of contact for shareholders — handles dividend payments, share transfers, proxy forms, and investor queries.

🔍

Due Diligence & M&A

Provides legal & secretarial support during mergers, acquisitions, restructuring, and due diligence processes.

🏷️

Corporate Governance Report

Prepares and publishes Corporate Governance Reports ensuring transparency and accountability to all stakeholders.

🔗

Regulatory Liaison

Maintains communication with SEBI, stock exchanges, RBI and other regulatory authorities on behalf of the company.

📜

Compliance Certificates

Certifies that the company has complied with all applicable laws, rules, and regulations during the financial year.

💼

Legal Representative

Acts as the company's legal representative and can appear before tribunals like NCLT on the company's behalf.

Thank You!

A Company Secretary is the backbone of good corporate governance.
